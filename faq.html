FAQ

The questions you should be asking.

Before you pay us or anyone else a penny, here are honest answers. If something is still unclear, that is what the call is for.

Do you guarantee I will get a funded PhD?

No. We guarantee the system and our guidance, never the outcome. Admission depends on your work, your field, your timing, and factors none of us control.

Anyone who guarantees you a PhD is not being honest with you. The fact that we will not is exactly why you can trust the rest of what we say.

How long does this actually take?

The programme itself is eight weeks. Building the profile that earns a funded place takes roughly twelve to eighteen months of your own consistent work after that.

It can be faster or slower depending on how much you put in. Around eighteen months is typical. The eight weeks gives you the map; the rest is the work, and it is yours to do.

I am already deep into my Graduate Route visa. Is it too late?

It depends on how much runway you have left. If you have a year or more, you are in good shape. If you have only a few months and have not started, we will tell you honestly on the call rather than take your money for a plan that cannot finish in time.

This works best for final-year bachelor's students, current master's students, and people freshly onto the Graduate Route.

Do I need to be technical or already good at research?

You need to be willing to learn and to do the work. The method rests on offering researchers real, useful help, so the more capable you become, the easier it gets. We will point you at what matters, but we are not here to do the work for you.

Why should I trust you over a visa agent or consultant?

Because we did this ourselves and we will not promise you the moon. We are not selling admissions or pulling strings. We are showing you a path we have actually walked, and being straight about the work it takes.

We also turn people away when the timing is wrong. An agent chasing a fee will not do that.

What exactly do I walk away with after eight weeks?

A target shortlist of researchers to approach and the templates to do it, a drafted academic CV, a first draft of your PhD proposal and personal statement, the supervisor funding-outreach playbook, and a mock interview with feedback that you bank for the application stage.

Real work in hand, not just notes.

How much does it cost?

We discuss cost privately, on the call, and only if we both decide the programme is a fit for you. The first step is a free 30-minute counselling call where we understand your situation and whether we can help.

We work with a limited number of people at a time, so the call is also where we decide together whether you are a good fit. If you are, we explain exactly how it works and what it costs, with no pressure either way.

What is your refund position?

Because the free onboarding call comes first, you only join the programme once we both agree it fits. If you change your mind, you can request a full refund within 7 days of joining, provided the core materials have not yet been delivered. We keep this simple and we honour it, no fine print, no chasing.
